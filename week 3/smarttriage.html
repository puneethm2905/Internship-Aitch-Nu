SMART TRIAGE ASSISTANT

IN EMERGENCY WARDS

Emergency wards are high-pressure environments where rapid and accurate triage is essential to save lives. Traditional triage processes, often based on manual paperwork and subjective clinical judgment, are prone to delays, inconsistencies, and fragmented decision-making. With patient data scattered across labs, radiology units, and monitoring devices, doctors may face difficulty in obtaining a holistic and timely view of a patient's condition.

The proposed project, Smart Triage Assistant in Emergency Wards, aims to overcome these barriers by introducing a web-based bedside system that automatically processes and analyzes multi-source patient data to generate real-time, reliable triage recommendations. Test results, medical history, and continuous monitoring updates are immediately stored in a centralized hospital database, eliminating paperwork and enabling seamless, effective, data-driven decision-making.

At the core of this intelligent system lies the powerful Random Forest machine learning algorithm. This model is exceptionally well-suited for handling heterogeneous medical data and complex relationships between variables. Random Forest provides stability, robustness, and interpretability by combining multiple decision trees, making it highly reliable for medical classification tasks.

The model allows the system to classify patients into categories such as Critical, Moderate, or Stable, with probability-based scoring to support confident and safe clinical decisions.

The triage outcomes are displayed on interactive, color-coded dashboards accessible at the patient's bedside. This visualization presents doctors with a clear overview of patient conditions across the ward, highlighting critical cases that need immediate intervention. By providing a reliable, automated assessment tool, the system supports clinicians in prioritizing patients fairly and quickly during crowded emergency scenarios.
